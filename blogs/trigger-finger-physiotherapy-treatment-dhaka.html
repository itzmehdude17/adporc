Trigger Finger (Stenosing Tenosynovitis): Causes, Symptoms & Physiotherapy Treatment in Jatrabari, Dhaka | ADPORC

Trigger Finger, medically known as stenosing tenosynovitis, is a painful hand condition where a finger gets stuck in a bent position and suddenly straightens with a snap. What is Trigger Finger?

Trigger finger happens when the flexor tendon in your finger becomes irritated, inflamed, or thickened. This prevents smooth tendon movement, leading to painful locking or catching of the finger. Nodules may also form on the tendon, worsening stiffness and discomfort.

Common Symptoms of Trigger Finger

  1. Finger stiffness, especially in the morning
  2. Pain when bending or straightening the finger
  3. A clicking or popping sound during movement
  4. Finger locking in a bent position
  5. Sudden painful straightening of the finger
  6. Persistent tenderness at the base of the affected finger

Common Causes & Risk Factors

  1. Repetitive gripping or hand strain
  2. Diabetes and thyroid disorders
  3. A clicking or popping sound during movement
  4. Rheumatoid arthritis (RA) or gout
  5. Previous hand injury
  6. More common in women aged 40-60

How Physiotherapy Helps in Trigger Finger

At ADPORC, our treatment focuses on restoring smooth finger movement, reducing inflammation, and avoiding surgery whenever possible. We offer-

  1. Finger stretching and tendon gliding exercises
  2. Splinting techniques to rest the tendon
  3. Manual therapy and soft tissue mobilization
  4. Advanced electrotherapy for pain relief
  5. ESWT (Extracorporeal Shock Wave Therapy) – a FDA-approved modern treatment approach

At ADPORC, Dr. Saddam and his team tailor plans for your specific condition and lifestyle, ensuring safe and effective recovery.

When to See a Physiotherapist

If your pain does not improve with rest, splints, or medication, consult a physiotherapist. Early physiotherapy can prevent long-term stiffness and the need for surgery.
